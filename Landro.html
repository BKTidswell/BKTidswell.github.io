Vassar Robotics Research

At Vassar College I am working on a cognitive science research project with a team of three students and three profesors. Our hypothesis is that gene duplication increases modularity of the networks, and modular networks give advantages to agents, improving the evolvability of the population. We created artificial genomes that dictate their our robotics agents neural networks. We created an arena with lights and walls, and reward the robots with increased fitness if they are able to find areas with lots of light away from the walls, or in darkness close to the walls. Through reproduction and mutation we expect later agents to be be able to better perform the task we designed for it. This research project is still ongoing.
