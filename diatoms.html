Oxidative Stress Genes in Diatoms

The purpose of our project was to learn more about the upstream regions of oxidative stress related genes in diatoms. Diatoms produce reactive oxygen species which are useful in small amounts but in high levels can damage DNA, proteins, and lipids. Our project focused on three specific promoter types, the TATA box, the GC box, and the CAT box. We BLASTed the genomes against known oxidative stress genes in mice and plants, and then located them in the diatom genomes. I provided the coding expertise for this project. We found that the two model species of diatom shared a larger percentage of oxidative stress related genes than they did other genes, but we did not find any consistant trends in their upstream regions.
